# Learner intake — why two phases (intelligence sequencing)

Readers often worry: **“How can intelligent questions exist before we know expertise?”**

They **can't** — not if “intelligent” means **respectful, calibrated, non‑generic.**

## The sequencing law

```
Part 1 captures self‑declared basics + claimed expertise anchors
               ↓
Part 2 (generated or facilitator‑authored follow‑ups conditioned on Part 1)
               ↓
Live intake or async completion
```

**Part 1** is intentionally **structured** free text: sectors, jurisdictions, three expertise buckets, protected jargon — enough signal for tailoring.

**Part 2** consumes that signal via:

- Paste + [`templates/learner-intake-followup-generator-prompt.md`](../templates/learner-intake-followup-generator-prompt.md), or  
- Human facilitator authoring using [`conversation-intelligence-prep.md`](conversation-intelligence-prep.md).

## Myths vs reality

| Myth | Reality |
|------|---------|
| “AI should amaze them cold.” | Calibration needs **explicit self‑reports** unless you possess **privileged** dossiers—you don’t want those. |
| “One heroic form solves everything.” | First pass is breadth; tailored questions add depth without wasting senior time upfront. |

## Elite track note

Elite respondents still complete **Part 1 first**; extra time goes into **human QA** of generated Part 2 + optional public desk context—never skipping self‑report on expertise.
