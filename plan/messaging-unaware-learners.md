# Finding leaders & SMEs who don’t yet know they need structure

Git4Gits serves people who often **haven’t articulated** their need: AI-assisted coding feels like a **sudden superpower**—fast wins, flashy demos—which can hide why **structured learning** matters. The standout outcome is still **domain experts wielding real leverage**—verifiably—not a certificate.

## Core insight

| Phase | What people feel | What they miss |
|-------|-------------------|----------------|
| **First contact** | “I’m shipping so fast”—euphoria | Scope creep, hallucinated confidence, no checks |
| **First bad surprise** | Distrust of AI *or* of self | No vocabulary for verification, escalation, rollback |
| **Stuck plateau** | “It works for demos, not Mondays” | No repeatable workflow (brief → implement → prove) |

**Positioning discipline:** Sell the **superpower** (outcome + identity), teach the **grammar** (structure) as *what keeps the superpower from backfiring*—not as school for its own sake.

## Who often doesn’t know they need Git4Gits yet

**Leaders**

- Sponsor “tool rollouts” (Copilot licenses) **without** workflow, data rules, or review bandwidth.
- Measure **usage / lines / PR count** instead of **defects caught, rework avoided, SMEs who can explain their diff**.
- Assume “engineers got this; SMEs can figure it out”—so **support budget** never appears.

**SMEs**

- Power users of Chat-style AI who think **coding is just longer prompts**.
- Brilliant with Excel/domain rules but never **named** acceptance criteria or tests.
- Afraid of looking “non-technical,” so they **hide** fragility until something breaks in front of a boss.

These people are **not wrong** to be excited—excitement is the hook. The gap is **unconscious need** for structure until pain or risk appears.

## Language that lands (without talking down)

| Avoid sounding like… | Prefer framing… |
|----------------------|-------------------|
| “You need remedial discipline.” | “You already carry the hardest part—the **domain**. We’re adding the **safety rail** so the superpower scales past Tuesday.” |
| “Process for process’s sake.” | “**Brief before code** is how you aim the superpower; **checks** are how you know you hit the target.” |
| “Trust the framework.” | “**Trust, but verify**—same as medicine, finance, law; AI is just faster at being wrong confidently.” |

**Leaders (one-liner):** *Your best SMEs are about to 10× output—only if we give them **guardrailed mastery**, not orphaned licenses.*

**SMEs (one-liner):** *Keep the thrill of building with AI; add three habits so nobody gets hurt and you can reuse what you shipped.*

## Signals in discovery (they don’t know they need structure—yet)

Use these in chats and intros; optional prompts live in [`interview-script.md`](interview-script.md).

- Says “Copilot writes most of my code now” **but cannot** explain what changed line-by-line.
- Ships to **prod-ish** surfaces without reviewer or rollback story.
- **No** repeatable place for prompts, data classification, or “definition of wrong.”
- Describes failures as **model bad** vs **workflow bad** (only the latter is coachable at scale).

When you spot this, **do not preach**—show one **verification win** + one **silent failure demo** (redacted).

## Outreach channels that reach the “unaware need” population

| Channel | Why it can work |
|---------|------------------|
| **Manager / staff meeting** (15 min) | Leader sees **risk + talent** in one frame; SMEs see peer permission. |
| **Lunch demo: “superpower + guardrail”** | Euphoria hook, then **one** live check (row count, diff review). |
| **Internal Communities of Practice** | Champions who overflow with tips but leak bad habits—invite them **in**, teach review ritual. |
| **Vendor launch train-the-trainer** | Ride Copilot onboarding; slip in **problem brief + capstone checklist** as “how we operationalize licenses here.” |

## What Git4Gits must deliver so messaging stays honest

Otherwise “superpower” marketing becomes **hollow hype**:

1. **One gold path** SMEs can replay (brief → artifact → checks)—see backlog in roadmap discussion (not yet fully built as session 2–3 pack).
2. **Synthetic wins** leaders can cite without leaking data.
3. **Capstone rubric** that names **verification**, not vibes—[`templates/capstone-checklist.md`](../templates/capstone-checklist.md).
